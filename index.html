How Nuclear Power Plants Work

Nuclear Fission

All nuclear power plants use nuclear fission, and most nuclear power plants use uranium atoms because it is one of the most unstable elements. During nuclear fission, a neutron collides with a uranium atom and splits it, releasing a large amount of energy in the form of heat and radiation. These neutrons continue to collide with other uranium atoms, and the process repeats itself over and over again.
